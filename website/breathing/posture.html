Posture

 

Re-evaluate Your Stance

Correct posture helps so stand tall, feet shoulder width apart. This allows you to breathe easily and to allow maximum lung capacity to allow better notes and phrases.

  • Stand up straight, shoulders back and down, floating over your torso. Make sure that your chest is high to give room for your lungs and intercostal muscles to expand and contract. It may help to imagine a string that connects to the ceiling holding your chest up.
  • Relax. Release tension from your body. Try stretching your arms, legs, torso, and neck. When stretching your neck avoid rolling your neck back to prevent tension and snapping your neck-just roll it from shoulder to shoulder.
  • If you're sitting, the same things apply! Keep both your feet flat on the floor--don't cross your legs. Keeping your body in line allows for more control and sustained singing without strain.
